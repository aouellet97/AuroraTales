Featured Experiences

Aurora Tour

Venture deep into the heart of the Arctic Circle to witness the vibrant hues of the northern lights. From cozy lodges to remote tundra landscapes, experience the Aurora in its full glory while learning about its science and folklore.

Wilderness Adventure

Explore the Arctic terrain and catch glimpses of majestic creatures like moose, wolves, and arctic foxes. As night falls, be enchanted by the dancing lights of the northern sky, creating a magical backdrop to your wilderness experience.

Frequently asked questions

If you have any questions or need further information, please don't hesitate to contact us. Our team is always available to assist you and ensure your journey with us is nothing short of extraordinary.

While we cannot guarantee a 100% chance of seeing the auroras due to weather conditions and other factors, our experienced guides will take you to prime viewing locations where the auroras are most likely to appear. Rest assured, we'll do everything possible to ensure you have the best opportunity to witness the magic of the Northern Lights during your trip with us.

The best time to see the Northern Lights is during the winter months when the nights are long and dark. In most locations, the prime viewing season is from late September to early April.

To photograph the Northern Lights, you'll need a camera with manual settings, a sturdy tripod, and a remote shutter release. Set your camera to a high ISO, wide aperture, and long exposure time to capture the vibrant colors of the Aurora.

Yes, traveling to the Arctic for a Northern Lights expedition is generally safe, but it's essential to follow the guidance of experienced guides, dress appropriately for the weather, and be prepared for changing conditions.
